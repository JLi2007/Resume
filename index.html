About Me
"You are already halfway there if you believe in yourself"

My name is James Li, and I'm going into eleventh grade at Laurel Heights Secondary School in Waterloo. Coming from an Asian background, I was taught early on that a successful future is built day-by-day by academics. The problem was that, though I always did excellent in school and always achieved high grades, I was lost when it came to my future. I love setting goals for myself, but when it came to my future, I felt stuck, like there was nothing for me to build towards. This all changed when I got more into CS. I took the Grade 11 CS course in the second semester of Grade 10, and all the wires connected one-by-one in my brain from there. My heavy interest, mixed with my strong hunger to learn, gave me something to finally work towards. The list is getting bigger by the month, but now I know Python, C, HTMl, CSS, and a bit of Javascript. It has been a great journey so far, but I am nowhere near the end. One huge regret I have is how many opportunities I have missed out on. Growing up, I was a quiet kid; thus, I did not have the confidence to join many clubs, groups, or participate in many events. This still partly shows today, as when it comes to larger groups and talking in front of a lot of people, I still struggle a bit. Around the start of 2023, when I finally realised the value of opportunities and I started signing up for many different types of events and groups, many of which I will talk about later in my resume. Though I wish I could have been more spontaneous and more open to things growing up, the past cannot be changed, and what is crucial now is for me to continue participating in events and in larger groups.

"Knowing is not enough, we must apply. Willing is not enough, we must do" - Bruce Lee

Anyone can learn, but applying the information that one has gathered is just as important; because otherwise, the compelling and deep knowledge gained will all eventually go to waste. Similarly, anyone can plan, or even express that they would like to work, but nothing would matter unless actual work is done. To me, this quote expresses the overlooked value of simply taking tasks hands-on instead of overthinking the details.

"It's not whether you get knocked down, its whether you get up." - Vince Lombardi

I think this motivational quote speaks for itself. It's okay to be knocked down by somebody else, it's okay to disappoint yourself, it's okay to make plenty of mistakes every day. It is how one rebounds from these situations that determines the outcome. Two decisions present themselves in these situations: quickly move on, or learn from whatever the problem was, then moving on.

"Victory is reserved for those who are willing to pay its price." - Sun Tzu

To be victorious, one must earn it. There are many ways one can interpret this, but to me, I see it from two perspectives. Playing rep soccer and now being involved in larger groups such as SVP teens, victory means achieving OUR goals, not just mine. So to earn it, collaboration is crucial. On the other hand, small victories must be won every single day to achieve success in the long run, "finishing my to-do list", or even "Finish my Resume Website" are great examples of my intriguing daily victories (which have not been won yet today, but we don't talk about that).
